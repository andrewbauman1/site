Scroll to the bottom of my homepage and you'll find a little black box with green digits: *Visitors ######*. No referral tracking, dashboard, or advanced analytics. It counts, and that's it.

I put it there on purpose.

When I started designing this site, I wanted it to feel like something from the early web. Not literally, of course. Nobody needs to sit through a dial-up handshake to read a blog post. But I wanted a little of that spirit to survive the redesign. Instead, what I actually built is cleaner and more modern than that mental image. Unfortunately, at least for now, there are no marquee tags or animated GIFs. The counter is what's left of the original idea, sitting quietly in the footer.

Some of it is nostalgia. I grew up taking computers apart and reverse engineering software just to see how they worked. My grandfather worked in systems for Bell Telephone, and he was one of my first real windows into that world. He programmed with punch cards and Apple BASIC. We met somewhere in the middle.
I ended up with his old Apple II, which I'll eventually write more about.

The counter brings me back to that era. Flash games, AIM, early Skype calls, Windows Media Player visualizers, and Clippy. Mostly, though, it's just reassurance. Proof I'm not writing into a void. Somebody's out there, and the number went up.

A real analytics dashboard would tell me more. Where you came from, what you clicked, how long you stayed. This counter doesn't know any of that and doesn't care. It just noticed you showed up.

So welcome. No matter whether you're visitor 000003, 99999, or somewhere in between, I'm glad you're here.
